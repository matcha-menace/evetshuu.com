Preface

The original Level 2 of Ai2U was a challenging prototype quickly put together by a single designer with minimum dev support. It was plagued by many issues that boiled down to:

  • Unclear player goals and feedback
  • Overcomplicated design
  • Heavy reliance on LLM for narrative guidance

This resulted in a messy level leaving many players confused. My job was to completely overhaul the level based on player feedback, while keeping the core theme and overall progression intact to minimize dev and art workload.

Process

The task sounded daunting but the steps to approach it were clear.

First, I conducted a whole day of extensive playtest to document any and all frictions I found in the level.

Then I wrote down my findings and broke them down into manageable talking points to address accordingly. I found that:

Issue

Unclear potion mechanics causing LLM to hallucinate Poorly generated maze making endgame content a chore to navigate Puzzles are scattered across the entire level, which are fully accessible from the start Puzzles are barely guided, which requires a lot of guessing and LLMs tend to be misleading One of the puzzles have a confusing instant kill effect, killing many runs

Solution

Add clues in level to help explain potion mechanics, and make the mechanic non-required for level progression Fully redesign the maze to make end goal clear with a landmark, and cinematic guidance Make part of the level hidden behind a simpler puzzle, ease the players into the puzzle progression Additional narrative cues that directly relate to the puzzle solutions Remove the instant kill effect and replace with a guide to help focus on level progression instead of this particular lore

After writing down each proposed solution, I began by creating a pitch for the team to both get everyone on the same page and to get feedback on scoping for the redesign.

After the short discussion, I adjusted each team member's tasks and planned out the development progress along with our Lead Designer.

Amaze

To help off-load dev team workload, I took on the maze rework myself. Working with art team, I designed the overall visuals of the maze, it's final layout, with a special touch of a landmark that can be seen through the fog to let the players know where their end goal is.

I reworked the maze generation code personally to add more variety in maze visuals and performance enhancements.

Reception

The entire rework took about 2 months in total from idea to ship. Once shipped, the players immediately praised the improved level and it helped in revitalizing the core player base.
